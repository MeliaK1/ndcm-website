Our people

Academics and specialists

Meet the academics and experts supporting the Natural Disasters and Crisis Management Research Center.

Costas Synolakis

Costas Synolakis is an internationally recognised specialist in tsunamis, coastal engineering and the effects of extreme natural hazards on coastlines. He studied engineering and civil engineering at the California Institute of Technology and has held academic positions at the University of Southern California and the Technical University of Crete.

His research combines wave theory, laboratory work, numerical modelling and field investigations after major tsunamis and coastal disasters. He has led or participated in scientific expeditions across many regions of the world and has contributed to tsunami hazard assessment, early-warning systems and coastal resilience.

Stamatios M. Krimigis

Stamatios M. Krimigis is a space physicist whose career has been closely connected with the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory and the exploration of the Solar System. He studied physics at the University of Minnesota and completed his doctorate at the University of Iowa under James Van Allen.

He has led or contributed to instruments on numerous NASA and European space missions, including Voyager, Cassini, New Horizons and Parker Solar Probe. His research focuses on planetary magnetospheres, the heliosphere and energetic particles in interplanetary space.

Athanasios Fokas

Athanasios Fokas is a mathematician and scientist known for his work in nonlinear mathematical science. He studied aeronautics at Imperial College London, completed a doctorate in applied mathematics at Caltech and later earned a medical degree from the University of Miami.

He has held senior academic appointments at Clarkson University, Imperial College London and the University of Cambridge. His research includes integrable systems, differential equations, mathematical physics and the unified transform method, widely known as the Fokas method.

Christos S. Zerefos

Christos S. Zerefos is an atmospheric physicist and climatologist whose work has focused on ozone, climate variability, solar effects on the atmosphere and environmental change. He studied physics, meteorology and physics-meteorology at the University of Athens.

He has led major academic and research initiatives in Greece, including the Research Centre for Atmospheric Physics and Climatology of the Academy of Athens, and has contributed to international scientific cooperation on climate and atmospheric research.

Andreas Karamanos

Andreas Karamanos is an agronomist and emeritus professor of crop production at the Agricultural University of Athens. He studied agronomy in Athens and completed doctoral studies in agricultural botany at the University of Reading.

During his academic career, he directed the Laboratory of Agronomy, led university departments and faculties, and served as Rector and Vice-Rector of the Agricultural University of Athens. His work covers crop production, agronomy and the relationship between agriculture and environmental conditions.

Emmanouil Floratos

Emmanouil Floratos is a mathematical physicist and professor emeritus of the University of Athens. His scientific work spans theoretical high-energy physics, quantum field theory, string theory, quantum information and mathematical structures in physics.

He has contributed extensively to research and teaching in nuclear and particle physics and has published widely across theoretical physics. He was elected to the Academy of Athens in the Chair of Mathematical Physics.

Georgios Marios Karagiannis

Georgios Marios Karagiannis is a civil engineer and specialist in emergency management, disaster risk management and critical infrastructure resilience. His academic background also includes postgraduate study in disaster management and business administration.

His professional experience includes work in civil protection, national risk assessment, crisis planning, emergency exercises and resilience policy. He has also worked on European and international projects dealing with critical infrastructure, emergency management and disaster preparedness.
