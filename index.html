About the Center

Science for resilient societies

The Center supports interdisciplinary research on natural hazards, disaster risk reduction, crisis response and civil protection. Its work bridges academic research, public policy and operational decision-making.

Research

Research areas

Wildfires

Research into wildfire behaviour, prevention, early warning, emergency response and the environmental and social consequences of major fire events.

Floods & Hydrometeorological Hazards

Assessment of flood risk, extreme rainfall, severe weather and their effects on communities, infrastructure and the natural environment.

Earthquakes & Geohazards

Study of seismic hazards, ground deformation, landslides and related geological processes, with an emphasis on preparedness and risk reduction.

Climate Change Adaptation

Development of evidence-based strategies that help institutions, cities and communities prepare for climate-related hazards and strengthen long-term resilience.
